Chapter 11 Treatment Planning I: Isodose Distribution and Plan Evaluation

11.1 Penumbra

The dose distribution outside the field boundaries is significantly affected by geometric penumbra, depth, leakage radiation through collimator. The flattening filter mostly affect dose within the field boundary.

The disucssion of penumbra can be found in Chapter 4.3.

11.2 Wedges

  • Physical wedge
    • External physical wedge
    • Internal physical wedge (a.k.a motorized wedge, as in ElektaTM machines) typically consists of a single large wedge (e.g., 60 degrees) placed above the secondary collimating jaws. The wedge field is form by combining the open (o) field and the 60o wedge field:
\[\begin{equation} \text{Dose}_{\theta}=W_{o}\text{Dose}_{o} + W_{60^o}\text{Dose}_{60^o}, \tag{11.1} \end{equation}\]

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad\) where \(W_{60^o}=\frac{tan\theta}{tan{60^o}}\).

  • Non-physical wedge
    • Virtual wedge (as in SiemensTM)
    • Enhanced dynamic wedge (EDW) in VarianTM, which is implemented by moving one of the collimating jaws from one end of the field to the other.

Wedge (isodose) angle is defined as the angle between wedged isodose curve (see figure wedge isodose) and the normal to the central axis at a specific depth (e.g., 10 cm). What we typically measure is wedge profile.

11.2.1 Wedge factors

According to Khan, the wedge (transmission) factor is defined as the ratios of dose measurement for a wedge field over that for an open field with the same field size and at the same depth along the central of the beam. It can be expressed as:

\[\begin{equation} \text{WF}=\frac{\text{D}_W(FS,d)}{\text{D}_O(FS,d)}. \end{equation}\]

The wedge factor required by the Pinnacle TPS is specifically defined as the ratio of the dose measurement for a wedge field and that for a open field (10 cm \(\times\) 10 cm) at the same depth of 10 cm: \[\text{WF}_{Pinnacle} = \frac{\text{D}_W(FS, 10\ cm)}{\text{D}_O(FS_c, 10\ cm)},\] where the subscription c stands for calibration.9 The sample 6 MV WFpinnacle can be seen in Table 11.1 (form reference 3).
