1 mR/hr = 1 mrem/hr for gamma and x-ray

The activity at which patients could be released was calculated by using, the method discussed in the NCRP Report No. 37, “Precautions in the Management of Patients Who Have Received Therapeutic Amounts of Radionuclides.”

\[\begin{equation} D(t)=34.6\times \frac {\Gamma Q_0 T_P \left(1-e^{-0.693/T_P}\right)} {r^2}, \end{equation}\]

where

  • 34.6 = Conversion factor of 24 hrs/day times the total integration of decay (1.44)
  • D(t) = accumulated exposure at time t, in R (It assumed that 1 R = 10 mSv = 1 rem)
  • \(\Gamma\) = Specific gamma ray constant for a point source, R/mCi-hr at 1 cm
  • \(Q_0\) = Initial activity of the point source in mCi, at the time of the release
  • \(T_P\) = Physical half-life in days
  • r = Distance from the point source to the point of interest, in cm
  • t = Exposure time in days

23.4 TheraSphere

Y-90 microsphere therapy usually target the liver, taking advantage of the unique circulatory system in the liver: portal vein (normal liver) and hepatic artery (tumor)22. The treatment is one of many trans-aterial (TA) treatments for the liver.

There are two types of microsphere therapy with different intents: TheraSphere® for hepatocelluar carcinoma and SIR-Spheres® for liver metastases from colorectal cancer. For TheraSphere®, the average activity per sphere of is 2,500 Bq (much higher than 70 Bq in SIR-Spheres®), and the average size of a glass sphere is about 25 \(\mu\)m. SIR-Spheres® is not discussed here but more detailed descriptions can be found from 2017 AAPM Annual meeting talk, Y90-Microsphere Therapy: Emerging Trends and Future Directions. ddcdab

Before treatment

To avoid radiation pnumanitis, the lung dose for TheraSphere should be less than 30 Gy. The lung shunt (LS) percentage can be calculated from the signals (counts) in Tc-99m (normally 2-4 mCi) MAA planar scintigraphy or SPECT/CT23,

\[\begin{equation} LS (\%) = \frac{\text{Lung Counts}}{\text{Lung Counts}+\text{Liver Counts}}\times 100 \tag{23.1} \end{equation}\]

where counts = (ANT counts\(\times\)POST counts)1/2.

Delineation of target volumes is based on digital segmentated angiography (DSA), CT, C-arm CBCT, SPECT/CT. The treatment volume is then converted to mass, using a conversion facotr of 1.03 g/cc. The required activity can then be calculated

\[ A_{Totoal} = A_{Liver} + A_{Lung}\] \[\begin{equation} D_{Lung} = \frac {50 (\text{J}/\text{GBq})\times A_{Lung}\times LSF} {M_{Lung}}. \tag{23.2} \end{equation}\] \[\begin{equation} D_{Liver} = \frac {50 (\text{J}/\text{GBq})\times A_{Lung}\times (1-LSF)} {M_{Liver}}. \tag{23.3} \end{equation}\]
